The Fundamental Three

Competitive Pokemon has many aspects, many of which are incredibly advanced. Understanding the deeper aspects of competitive Pokemon comes with many years of experience and intuition. However, if we look closer, we can see that the theories of competitive Pokemon arise from these three Fundamental Aspects: Type, Stats, and Abilities. These three aspects are the foundation of any competitive team or player, and knowledge of these aspects are non-negotiable when progressing through the ranks.

The Type Chart

The type is one of the most important concepts in competitive play, as it is a defining characteristic of a Pokemon's performance in battle. A Pokemon's type determines its strengths and weaknesses, which in turn directly influence a Pokemon's damage input and output. There are 18 different types in total, and each type has its own strengths and weaknesses against others. Because of the type matchup, moves against other Pokemon may be "super effective" (dealing significantly more damage), "not very effective" (dealing significantly less damage), or not effective at all (dealing no damage.)

A pokemon's type may also provide a general description of a pokemon's nature and stats. For instance, some types are known for their high durability, some types are chosen for high attack and speed, some types have abilities that are meta-defining, and some types have great variety in resistances against other types.

White tiles show a neutral relationship (same amount of damage), while green tiles show a super-effective relationship, and red tiles show a not very-effective relationship. Black tiles signify a not-at-all effective relationship, indicating that the damage dealt by a move will be nullified because of the type matchup involved in a battle.

Familiarizing oneself with the types and type matchups in Pokemon is non-negotiable. Because of the significant damage boosts/debuffs type matchups can give, depending on the type(s) of Pokemon in a team, the tide of battle may range from a guaranteed win to a quick loss. Knowing the meta is not enough for competitive play. Players with a strong understanding of types and type matchups will have an easier time building effective teams and therefore maximizing their chances of winning in battle.

Stats

Every Pokemon has six stats that define its capabilities in battle. These are namely Hit Points (also known as HP, which is how much damage a Pokemon can take before fainting), Attack (how much damage a Pokemon can deal with physical moves), Defense (how much damage a Pokemon can resist from physical moves), Special Attack (how much damage a Pokemon can deal with special moves), Special Defense (how much damage a Pokemon can resist from special moves), and Speed (determines which Pokemon moves first in battle).

Additionally, every species of Pokemon has their base stats. These are stats that a Pokemon is "born with," and they essentially determine a Pokemon's preferred role on the battlefield. They are the same for all Pokemon of a species. For instance, Garchomp has high speed and attack base stats, while Blissey has high HP. These pokemon have exceptional base stats; and therefore they have become popular options in competitive play.

IVs

However, base stats are not the only factor that determines a Pokemon's stats. Every Pokemon has Individual Values (IVs), which are hidden values that range from 0 to 31 for each stat. IVs are fixed and cannot be changed once a Pokemon is born, similar to base stats. These values are randomly generated when a Pokemon is encountered or bred, and they determine how much a Pokemon's stats will increase as it levels up. Higher IVs in a stat will result in higher stats for that specific stat. The highest IV value is 31, which is preferred for competitive matchup.

EVs

Every Pokemon also has Effort Values (EVs). Unlike IVs or Base Stats, EVs may be changed through various methods such as training, fighting, etc, hence the name: "Effort" Values. On the left, the EV distribution of the Turtwig remains small, meaning it hasn't specialized in any aspect of combat. However, on the right, the EV distribution of the Garchomp is highly skewed to the Attack stat. The high value of EVs in the Attack stat significantly boosts the Garchomp's damage output.

Abilities

An ability in Pokemon is a special attribute unique to each species of Pokemon. Abilities have the potential to greatly affect the outcome of battle. Some abilities give debuffs to opposing Pokemon, some abilities give boosts to the Pokemon possessing it, and others may alter the environment of the battlefield. There are a few common types of abilities Pokemon may have, namely:

Type of Ability Examples Description
Offensive Huge Power, Sheer Force Boosts a Pokemon's damage output for the battle.
Defensive Sturdy, Immunity Reduces damage taken
Support Intimidate, Friend Guard Provide utility effects such as stat lowering, etc.
Weather-Based Drizzle, Drought Gain benefits by changing the weather condition/s
Environment-Changing Grassy Surge, Misty Surge Create effects from the terrain changes
Healing Regenerator, Natural Cure Recovers a portion of health under specific circumstances
